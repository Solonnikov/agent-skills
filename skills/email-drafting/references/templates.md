# Templates

Starting points for common situations. Customize for the specific recipient.

## Cold outreach (getting a meeting)

```
Subject: 15 min on [SPECIFIC_TOPIC] this week?

Hi [NAME],

[ONE-SENTENCE REASON YOU'RE EMAILING THEM SPECIFICALLY — referenced work of theirs, mutual context, specific thing they said.]

I'm [NAME] at [COMPANY]; we [ONE-SENTENCE WHO WE ARE].

Could we grab 15 minutes this week to talk about [SPECIFIC, NARROW TOPIC]? I'd propose Thursday 2pm or Friday 10am your time; happy to work around.

[SIGNATURE]
```

Rules:
- Reference something specific about them — not generic flattery.
- Propose two times; don't ask "when are you free?"
- Name a length (15 min). Respects their time.

## Warm intro (double opt-in preferred)

```
Subject: Intro: [NAME_A] ←→ [NAME_B]

Hi both,

[NAME_A], meet [NAME_B]. [ONE-SENTENCE ON WHO B IS AND WHY A SHOULD CARE.]

[NAME_B], meet [NAME_A]. [ONE-SENTENCE ON WHO A IS AND WHY B SHOULD CARE.]

I'll step back now — over to you.

[SIGNATURE]
```

Rules:
- Before sending, ask both sides if they want the intro. Forwarding an unexpected intro is rude.
- Give each person a reason to reply. Don't just paste bios.

## Follow-up (no response)

```
Subject: Re: [ORIGINAL_SUBJECT]

Hi [NAME],

Bumping this up in case it got buried. No rush if now's not the time — happy to revisit in a few weeks.

[OPTIONAL: one-sentence reminder of what you were proposing.]

[SIGNATURE]
```

Rules:
- Short. Shorter than the original.
- Give them an easy out — "no rush if now's not the time".
- One follow-up, maybe two. Three is harassment.

## Apology (something you broke)

```
Subject: [SPECIFIC BROKEN THING]

Hi [NAME],

I [DID THE THING], and [IMPACT — what they experienced]. That's on me.

Here's what I'm doing to make it right: [CONCRETE ACTION, WITH TIMELINE].

To prevent this happening again: [CONCRETE PROCESS CHANGE].

Happy to talk it through if useful.

[SIGNATURE]
```

Rules:
- No "I'm sorry you felt...". Apologize for the action, not their reaction.
- Include what you're doing about it. Apologies without action are just words.
- Don't over-apologize. State it, fix it, move on.

## Thank-you

```
Subject: Thank you

Hi [NAME],

Thank you for [SPECIFIC THING — not "everything"].

[ONE SPECIFIC THING THAT MATTERED TO YOU OR HELPED.]

[OPTIONAL: anything you'd offer back, without being transactional.]

[SIGNATURE]
```

Rules:
- Specific > effusive. "Thanks for the reference to X — used it this week" beats "Thanks for everything!"
- Don't ask for something in the same email as a thank-you.

## Decline

```
Subject: Re: [ORIGINAL_SUBJECT]

Hi [NAME],

Thank you for thinking of me. I can't take this on — [OPTIONAL ONE-SENTENCE REASON].

[IF RELEVANT: suggest a connection or alternative.]

Best,
[SIGNATURE]
```

Rules:
- Short. Don't explain in paragraphs; it reads as justifying yourself.
- No maybe-later unless you mean it.
- Offering a connection ("you might want to talk to X") is generous but not required.

## Difficult feedback

```
Subject: [NEUTRAL TOPIC, NOT "feedback"]

Hi [NAME],

I wanted to share some thoughts on [WORK / SITUATION].

[THE OBSERVATION — specific, behavior not character, with an example.]

[THE IMPACT — what it affected.]

[WHAT YOU'D LIKE TO SEE — specific, actionable.]

Happy to talk it through — the goal is [SHARED GOAL].

[SIGNATURE]
```

Rules:
- In writing is usually second-best to a conversation. Prefer live feedback when possible; use email for a follow-up summary.
- Behavior, not character. "When X happened" not "You always".
- End with shared goal or next step. Not a vacuum.

## Ask for a reference / recommendation

```
Subject: Ask: reference for [TARGET]

Hi [NAME],

I'm applying for [ROLE] at [COMPANY], and would really appreciate a reference if you're able.

[IF HELPFUL: one sentence on what they'd highlight — since they worked with you on X.]

Short notice, no pressure. Application deadline is [DATE].

[OPTIONAL: attach résumé / JD for context.]

Thank you,
[SIGNATURE]
```

Rules:
- Give them an out (busy people are busy).
- Make their job easy — mention what they'd highlight, attach the JD.
- Give a deadline so they know when to act.

## Out of office / delegation

```
Subject: Out of office: back [DATE]

Hi,

I'm out of office until [DATE]. For urgent questions, please reach [COLLEAGUE_NAME] ([COLLEAGUE_EMAIL]).

I'll respond to non-urgent email when I'm back.

[SIGNATURE]
```

Rules:
- Date, not duration. "Back Monday, May 8" beats "Back in a week".
- Name a fallback for urgent.
- Don't promise to catch up on everything — you won't.

## Chase a non-response, escalating

First nudge (day 3–5 after original):

```
Subject: Re: [ORIGINAL_SUBJECT]

Hi [NAME], bumping this up — do you have 5 minutes to reply, or should I check back next week? [SIGNATURE]
```

Second (week 2):

```
Subject: Re: [ORIGINAL_SUBJECT]

Hi [NAME], following up once more. If this isn't the right time, a quick "try me in October" works. Otherwise I'll assume a no and let it rest. [SIGNATURE]
```

After that, let it rest. Further chases burn the relationship.
