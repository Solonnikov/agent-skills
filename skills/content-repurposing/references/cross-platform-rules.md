# Cross-platform rules

Each platform has native conventions. Violate them and engagement dies, even if the content is good.

## X / Twitter

### Voice

- Terse. Sentence fragments OK. One idea per post.
- Specific over vague. Numbers, examples, timestamps.
- Dry humor lands; earnest inspiration falls flat unless very specific.

### Length

- 280 chars. X Premium extends, but shorter posts get shared more.
- Thread for anything longer; don't write long single posts.

### Hashtags

- 0–1 hashtags max. Multiple hashtags = spam signal. Only use one if it's the specific topic name (#rustlang, #threejs).

### Emojis

- Sparse. 0–2 per post. An emoji where it's load-bearing (flag for a country, arrow for a sequence) is fine; decorative emojis look amateur.

### Links

- Link in the LAST post of a thread. Putting a link in post 1 drops engagement on the thread.
- X deprioritizes posts with links. Threads bury the link strategically.

### What gets engagement

- Specific, numbered lists ("5 patterns I use daily").
- Behind-the-scenes / unpolished-seeming content ("draft of my next post").
- Counterintuitive takes on well-known topics.
- Screenshots of code, charts, or data — visual hook.
- Quote-tweets of interesting posts + your take.

### What doesn't

- Self-promotional plugs without content.
- Broad takes on big topics ("AI will change everything").
- Over-emoji, over-hashtag posts.
- Walls of text without line breaks.

## LinkedIn

### Voice

- Warmer than X. First-person, personal framing welcome.
- "What I learned" / "A story from my work" formats do well.
- Professional but not corporate. Not "synergistic solutions"; not "my bro I just closed a deal".

### Length

- Visible preview is ~210 chars; "see more" truncates. Strong hook in first 2 lines.
- Long posts (up to 1,300 chars) perform well if the content warrants it.
- Use line breaks generously. Walls of text get scrolled past.

### Hashtags

- 3–5 hashtags at the bottom. LinkedIn's algorithm uses them for discovery.
- Mix popular (#leadership, 50M+ followers) with specific (#svelte, 50k followers) — Goldilocks for reach.

### Emojis

- More accepted than X, still sparse. ✅ for checklist items, 🚀 for launch — OK. Mostly stick to text.

### Links

- LinkedIn deprioritizes posts with external links (wants you to stay on-platform). Strategies:
  - Put the link in the first comment ("Full post 👇 in the comments.")
  - Use a shortened URL at the end, after the main content.
  - Embed media (image, video, document) instead of linking — higher engagement.

### What gets engagement

- Personal reflection posts with a clear lesson.
- Career stories (promotions, failures, pivots).
- Deep-dives with a clear pedagogical intent.
- Numbered lists of takeaways.
- Behind-the-scenes from companies / projects people have heard of.

### What doesn't

- Pure promotion.
- "Hot takes" without grounding.
- Cross-posts from X that haven't been adapted (voice is wrong).
- Polls with no follow-up content.

### "LinkedIn-speak" — avoid

- "I'm humbled and grateful..."
- "Excited to announce..."
- "Blessed to be on this journey..."
- "Here's why..."
- "Agree?"

These read as template and deflate engagement. Write like a person.

## Threads (Meta)

### Voice

- Casual. Closer to SMS than LinkedIn.
- More irreverent than X currently is (varies over time).
- Less polished is fine; drafts and fragments welcome.

### Length

- 500 chars per post.
- Chain multi-post threads; 2–5 posts is normal, more is rare.

### Hashtags

- Low tolerance. Zero or one. Threads community reacts to overt hashtagging as spammy.

### Emojis

- More welcome than X or LinkedIn. Mid-sentence ✨ flourishes, reaction emojis, etc.
- Still don't stack them.

### Links

- Links work but get less engagement than pure text.
- Threads algo favors conversation over clicks.

### What gets engagement

- Specific, narrow observations.
- Real-time reactions to current events / trends.
- Questions that invite response.
- Short "this is what I'm working on" posts.

### What doesn't

- Long lists.
- Corporate voice.
- Cross-posts from LinkedIn.

## Substack / Newsletter platforms

Covered in detail in [blog-to-newsletter.md](./blog-to-newsletter.md).

Quick rules:
- Personal direct-address voice.
- Subject line under 50 chars, ideally specific.
- Length: 500–1500 words is the sweet spot.
- One CTA per issue.

## Medium

- Use tags (5 max); they drive categorization.
- Long form acceptable (1500–3000 words).
- Format with headers, sub-headers, pull quotes.
- Paywall / member-only options — decide per post.
- Cross-post detection: original source preferred; canonical links to your blog if cross-posting.

## YouTube / Shorts

### Long-form video

- Thumbnail + title do 80% of the click work.
- Hook in the first 15 seconds — most viewers drop before a minute.
- Clear structure with chapters helps retention.

### Shorts / Reels / TikTok

- **Hook in 3 seconds** — the first frame matters most.
- 30–60 seconds total.
- Vertical 9:16.
- Captions mandatory (70% of viewers watch muted).
- Clear one-takeaway per video; don't try to teach three things.

## Cross-posting schedule

Don't blast the same content at all platforms simultaneously. Stagger:

- **Blog post**: Monday.
- **X thread**: Tuesday AM.
- **LinkedIn post**: Wednesday AM.
- **Threads**: sprinkle through the week as conversational follow-ups.
- **Newsletter**: Thursday (highest open rate day in most niches).
- **Short video**: Friday or the following Monday.

Reaches different audiences; doesn't burn the same audience twice.

## Quick reference

| Platform | Sweet-spot length | Hashtags | Emojis | Voice |
|----------|------------------|----------|--------|-------|
| X | 280 chars (thread for more) | 0–1 | 0–2 | Terse, dry, specific |
| LinkedIn | ~1,000 chars | 3–5 | Sparse | First-person, professional warm |
| Threads | 500 chars (chain for more) | 0–1 | More welcome | Casual, irreverent |
| Newsletter | 500–1,500 words | n/a | Sparse | Direct address, personal |
| Medium | 1,500–3,000 | 5 tags | Sparse | Long-form, structured |
| Shorts | 30–60 sec | In caption | Welcome | Quick, visual, one idea |
